Description of the Model

Hill's equation is used to model the expression of the gene of interest. It uses the central dogma of molecular biology to determine that the concentration of the protein in the living system is proportional to the amount of mRNA expressed by the organism, which in turn is proportional to the amount of the Gene available in the organism (viable genetic information for reading by the transcription machinery).
